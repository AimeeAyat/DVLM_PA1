Proxy BPD (bits-per-dimension)

It is an approximate measure of how well the model models the data distribution.

It is computed from the training loss (e.g., L_simple in diffusion models) and converted into bits per data dimension (pixel).

It represents how many bits the model needs to encode each pixel of the data.

Meaning of the value:

Lower BPD → better density estimation, meaning the model assigns higher probability to real data and models the dataset more accurately.
